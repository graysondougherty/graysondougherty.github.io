DJ Billybool Drops Album, DYR

DJ Billybool's new album DYR is a sonic immersion into the dreamlike mythology of Drain Gang aesthetics, combining ethereal soundscapes with hyper-surreal visuals. The project channels a spectral energy through airy synths, icy melodies, and distorted, floating vocal samples, all woven together to create a deeply atmospheric experience. With each track, Billybool cultivates a space that feels both spiritually transcendent and emotionally subterranean, inviting listeners to drift between mysticism and melancholy.

Visually, DYR is just as ambitious. The album artwork features an enigmatic, robed figure framed by gothic arches and glowing butterflies, iconography that echoes themes of rebirth, digital fantasy, and spiritual armor. As both a tribute to and evolution of the Drain Gang universe, DYR doesn't just align with the aesthetic, it expands it, offering a portal into a world where sound, vision, and mood blur into a single drifting entity.

Musically, DYR plays with contrast and texture, layering crystalline tones over murky, slow-moving basslines. There's a deliberate looseness to the structure of each track, as if they're less songs and more weather patterns passing through some alien sky. The album feels less concerned with narrative and more focused on atmosphere, creating a kind of emotional vapor that lingers long after the final note.

What sets DYR apart is its commitment to a fully realized aesthetic world, one that's not afraid to be both fragile and confrontational. DJ Billybool blends digital melancholy with baroque symbolism, crafting an experience that feels at once ancient and futuristic. It's not just an album to listen to, but one to inhabit, like a gothic cathedral constructed from sound and vapor.

DYR also resonates with the broader legacy of Drain Gang, building on the atmospheric groundwork laid by Thaiboy Digital's Legendary Member and Bladee's Exeter and 333. Like those records, it embraces vulnerability and spirituality, using sonic minimalism to express emotional maximalism. DJ Billybool's approach feels like a natural extension of this lineage, translating their otherworldly tone into something even more immersive and textural. In doing so, he positions DYR not just as a standalone project, but as a continuation of the collective's evolving mythology.

Yung Lean Goes on Tour

Yung Lean has announced his Forever Yung Tour in support of his new album Jonatan, released on May 2, 2025. The tour begins in October across North America, with shows in cities like LA, Brooklyn, and Toronto. In November, it heads to Europe, hitting major stops including Berlin, London, and Paris.

Bladee Goes on Tour

Bladee has unveiled his 2025 Martyr World Tour, supporting his recent EP Ste the Beautiful Martyr 1st Attempt and the 2024 album Cold Visions. The tour kicks off in July with shows across Australia, then resumes in October for a North American leg, hitting cities like Brooklyn, Los Angeles, and Toronto. It concludes in December with performances in Europe and the UK, including stops in London, Dublin, and Manchester.

One in A Million Video Drops

Bladee's new video for “One in a Million” shows him drifting through a misty graveyard, sword in hand, evoking themes of martyrdom and rebirth. The track's airy production by Lusi and Woesum adds to its dreamlike, saintly atmosphere.

New Song, MAKKA

Fakemink's new single “MAKKA,” featuring Ecco2k and produced by Mechatok, blends dreamy guitar loops with punchy basslines, creating a euphoric, genre-blurring sound. The accompanying video adds a surreal, DIY charm to this underground anthem.

The End of YEAR0001

Yung Lean and Bladee have officially parted ways with their longtime label, YEAR0001. They now release music independently through their own labels—World Affairs and Trash Island—signaling a new chapter of artistic autonomy.
